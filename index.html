Peles Castle:
Built in the 19th century as a summer residence for Romanian royalty, Peles Castle is a stunning example of Neo-Renaissance architecture. Filled with intricate wood carvings, lavish furniture, and beautiful art collections, it's a must-see for any history or architecture buff.

Bran Castle: Nicknamed "Dracula's Castle" due to its imposing presence and connection to Bram Stoker's gothic novel, Bran Castle is a 14th-century fortress with a rich history. Explore its towers, courtyards, and exhibits on medieval life.

Transylvania: Transylvania is a historical region in Romania known for its dramatic scenery, charming medieval towns, and fortified churches. It's also shrouded in legend, inspiring Bram Stoker's Dracula and countless other tales of vampires and monsters.
